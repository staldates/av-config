# Director Role

## Overview

**Location:** Gallery / control position

**Primary responsibility:** Visual leadership of the service and camera switching.

The Director controls the **Cam Mix ME** on the ATEM control panel and coordinates the camera team. This role decides which camera is live and guides camera operators to frame the next shot.

## Key Responsibilities

- Switch cameras during the service using the ATEM 1 ME Advanced Panel
- Communicate with camera operators via comms
- Call shots and prepare the next camera
- Coordinate with the Graphics / IMAG Producer so the room screens and cameras work well together
- Maintain awareness of what is happening on stage

## Equipment Used

- **ATEM 1 ME Advanced Panel:** Physical control surface for camera switching
- **Comms System:** Communication with camera operators
- **Monitoring:** Multiple screens showing all camera feeds and program output

## Pre-Service Checklist

- [ ] Check all camera feeds are visible on monitors
- [ ] Test comms system with all camera operators
- [ ] Verify ATEM panel is controlling the Cam Mix ME
- [ ] Review service order/running sheet
- [ ] Coordinate with other team members on any special requirements

## During Service

### Camera Switching Guidelines

1. **Follow the action:** Switch to cameras capturing the current focus of the service
2. **Use cameras by role:**
   - Camera 1 for close-ups of individuals
   - Camera 3 for wider shots and when two people are on stage
   - Camera 5 for variety and dynamic angles (avoid congregation)
3. **Vary angles:** Mix wide shots, medium shots, and close-ups for visual interest
4. **Use fixed cameras strategically:**
   - Camera 2 (wide shot) for safety and establishing shots
   - Camera 4 (drums) for energy during worship
   - Camera 6 (hosts) for online-specific segments
5. **Give operators time:** Call next shots early enough for operators to prepare
6. **Stay smooth:** Avoid rapid switching unless called for by the moment
7. **Respect privacy:** Avoid congregation shots (Camera 2 wide is acceptable)

### Communication Tips

- Use clear, concise directions: "Camera 1, tight on speaker"
- Give advance notice: "Camera 3, prepare for wide worship shot"
- Acknowledge good shots: Brief positive feedback builds team confidence
- Stay calm: Your tone sets the atmosphere for the whole team

## Skills That Help

- Quick reactions to what is happening on screen
- Clear communication
- Comfort using control panels or controllers
- Ability to anticipate the next moment in the service

## Common Scenarios

### Worship Time

- Mix between wide shots (Camera 2), drums (Camera 4), and worship leader close-ups
- Camera 1: Close-ups of individual worship leaders and vocalists
- Camera 3: Wider shots of worship team, use when two vocalists are singing together
- Camera 5: Detail shots of instruments, dynamic angles
- Follow the energy - more cuts during upbeat songs, hold shots during reflective moments

### Teaching/Preaching

- Camera 1: Primary close-up shots of the speaker
- Camera 3: Wider shots when two people are on stage (interviews, discussions)
- Camera 5: Alternative angles and variety
- Camera 2: Wide shots for context and scene-setting
- Hold shots longer - fewer cuts during teaching
- Avoid congregation reaction shots for privacy

### Announcements

- Medium to close shots of the person speaking
- Cut to video playback when needed
- Coordinate with Stream Producer for overlays
