# IMAG Director Role (Non-Streamed Services)

## Overview

**Location:** Back of the room

**Primary responsibility:** Managing everything that appears on the in-room screens during non-streamed services.

**Key difference from streamed services:** You work independently, making all decisions about camera switching, graphics timing, and visual presentation. There is no separate Director or production team.

## What You're Managing

- **Camera switching:** Deciding when to show Camera 1, Camera 3, or black background
- **ProPresenter operation:** Triggering lyrics, slides, and teaching content
- **Service flow:** Following the service independently and making quick decisions
- **In-room experience:** Focus on what helps the congregation in the room

## Equipment Used

- **Stream Deck:** Quick access controls for IMAG ME camera switching (connected via Bitfocus Companion)
- **ProPresenter (IMAG/Graphics machine):** Presentation software for lyrics and slides
- **Computer/Monitors:** Operating the IMAG and ProPresenter systems

## Camera Configuration

**Typical Setup:**

- **Camera 1 operator:** Provides live shots of speakers, band, stage activity
- **Camera 3:** Locked on a general wide or semi-wide shot (no operator)
- You switch between these cameras, or show black background with text

**Camera Switching Decisions:**

- Use Camera 1 for all speaking (teaching, announcements, readings)
- Use locked Camera 3 sparingly - only if needed for variety
- Show black background during music/worship (lyrics only, no camera)

## Pre-Service Checklist

- [ ] Launch ProPresenter and load the service playlist
- [ ] Check Stream Deck is connected and controlling IMAG ME
- [ ] Verify in-room screens are displaying correctly
- [ ] Review service order and identify all slides/songs needed
- [ ] Check Camera 1 feed is available and clear
- [ ] Verify Camera 3 is locked on appropriate wide shot (if being used)
- [ ] Test camera switching on Stream Deck
- [ ] Confirm you're ready to work independently

## During Service

### General Approach

**You're making all the decisions.** Stay ahead of the service flow, anticipate transitions, and make quick choices about what to show on screens.

**Priorities:**

1. Follow the service order closely
2. Keep lyrics/slides visible when needed
3. Show speakers clearly on camera
4. Make smooth transitions
5. Stay calm and adaptable

### Music / Worship

**Display lyrics on black background (no camera feed)**

- Keep text visible throughout the entire song
- Simple, clean presentation focused on words
- No camera switching during songs
- Black background helps congregation focus on worship, not the stage

**ProPresenter Operation:**

1. Stay one step ahead in the worship playlist
2. Trigger next slide in time with the music
3. Keep lyrics visible until song ends
4. Transition smoothly between songs

### Teaching / Preaching

**Show Camera 1 as the primary shot**

- Switch to Camera 1 before speaker begins
- Keep Camera 1 visible throughout teaching
- Display teaching slides when speaker references them
- Switch back to Camera 1 when slides are no longer needed
- Minimize text on camera - only show slides when necessary

**Decision Points:**

- Does the speaker need this slide visible?
- How long should it stay up?
- When do I switch back to camera only?

**Tips:**

- Watch the speaker for cues about when slides are referenced
- Don't leave slides up too long after speaker moves on
- Clear communication is priority - show what helps people follow

### Announcements

**Show Camera 1 for speaker**

- Display announcement slides when directed
- Switch between camera and slides as needed
- May include video playback
- Return to camera when announcements complete

### Readings / Liturgy

**Choose based on context:**


- **Option 1:** Show Camera 1 with reader visible
- **Option 2:** Display text on black background (like worship lyrics)
- **Decision factors:** Length of reading, whether congregation is reading along, leadership preference

## Skills That Help

- Comfort using computers
- Ability to follow the flow of the service
- Quick decision-making when working independently
- Attention to detail and timing
- Calm under pressure

## Common Challenges

### "I'm not sure when to switch"

**Trust your instincts.** If the speaker is referencing a slide, show it. When they're done with it, go back to camera. You're trying to help the congregation follow what's happening.

### "The service isn't following the order"

**Stay flexible.** Services don't always go exactly as planned. Watch what's actually happening and adapt. Better to show the right thing at the right time than to stick rigidly to a plan.

### "I showed the wrong slide"

**Fix it quickly and move on.** Everyone makes mistakes. Jump to the correct slide and continue. The congregation is focused on the service, not minor technical glitches.

### "Should I use Camera 3?"

**Usually no.** Camera 1 with an operator is nearly always better than locked Camera 3. Only use Camera 3 if you specifically need a wide shot that Camera 1 can't provide at that moment.

## Troubleshooting

### ProPresenter Issues

**ProPresenter won't launch:**

- Restart the computer
- Check ProPresenter license is active

**Graphics not showing on screens:**

- Check IMAG ME upstream key is enabled
- Verify ProPresenter output settings
- Check physical cable connections

### Camera Switching Issues

**Stream Deck not responding:**

- Check USB connection
- Restart Bitfocus Companion software
- Verify Stream Deck is configured for IMAG ME

**Camera feed not showing:**

- Check camera is powered on
- Verify ATEM input connections
- Check cable connections

**Camera 1 has problem (blocked, poor framing, technical issue):**

- Switch to locked Camera 3 temporarily
- Use text slides on black background if appropriate
- Alert Camera 1 operator via comms or in person

### Coordination Issues

**Camera 1 operator absent or late:**

- You can run with locked Camera 3 only if necessary
- Use black background with slides for maximum flexibility
- Consider postponing camera switching if service allows
