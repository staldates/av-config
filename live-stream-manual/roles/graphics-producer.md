# Graphics / IMAG Producer Role

## Overview

**Location:** Back of the room

**Primary responsibility:** Managing what appears on the in-room screens.

This role runs **IMAG ME** using a Stream Deck and operates **ProPresenter** for lyrics, slides, and visual elements in the room.

**Note:** Responsibilities differ significantly between streamed and non-streamed services (see below).

## Key Responsibilities

### During Streamed Services

- Operate ProPresenter for lyrics, slides, and lower thirds
- Produce lower thirds and graphics that key over the camera feed from the Director's Cam Mix ME
- Coordinate with the Director and Stream Producer as part of the production team
- Trigger visual elements synchronized with the service

### During Non-Streamed Services

- Work independently (no Director or Stream Producer)
- Operate ProPresenter for lyrics and slides
- Switch cameras using Stream Deck (primarily Camera 1 for speaking)
- Display song words on black background during music
- Show Camera 1 during speaking segments

## Equipment Used

- **Stream Deck:** Quick access controls for IMAG ME switching (connected via Bitfocus Companion)
- **ProPresenter (IMAG/Graphics machine):** Presentation software with dual outputs (key + fill for both IMAG and Stream)
- **Computer/Monitors:** Operating the IMAG and ProPresenter systems

**ProPresenter Output Configuration:**
- 2 outputs, each with key and fill signals
- Routed to upstream keys (USKs) on IMAG ME and Stream ME
- IMAG output uses larger text for in-room viewing
- Stream output uses smaller, subtitle-style text
- This machine handles service lyrics, slides, and teaching content

## Pre-Service Checklist

### For All Services

- [ ] Launch ProPresenter and load the service playlist
- [ ] Check Stream Deck is connected and controlling IMAG ME
- [ ] Verify in-room screens are displaying correctly
- [ ] Review service order and identify all slides/songs needed
- [ ] Test ProPresenter key and fill outputs on IMAG ME

### Streamed Services Only

- [ ] Verify both IMAG and Stream outputs are working in ProPresenter
- [ ] Coordinate with Director on timing and graphics
- [ ] Coordinate with Stream Producer on lower thirds timing

### Non-Streamed Services Only

- [ ] Check Camera 1 feed is available and clear
- [ ] Test camera switching on Stream Deck
- [ ] Prepare to work independently

## During Service

### Streamed Services

**Key Concept:** During streamed services, camera feeds are the background all the time. You're adding graphics and text that key over the Director's camera mix.

#### ProPresenter Operation

1. **Follow the service flow:** Stay one step ahead in the playlist
2. **Timing:** Trigger lyrics, slides, and lower thirds at the right moment
3. **Key graphics over video:** Your ProPresenter output keys over the camera feed from Cam Mix ME
4. **Coordinate closely:** Work with Director for timing of graphics
5. **Be ready to adapt:** Services don't always go exactly as planned

#### Lower Thirds Production

- Produce lower thirds that overlay the camera feed
- Include names, titles, information overlays
- Time them appropriately with what's happening on camera
- Coordinate with Director on when to trigger

#### Communication

- Stay in contact with the Director
- Coordinate with Stream Producer on timing
- Alert team if there are technical issues
- Be ready to adjust on the fly

### Non-Streamed Services

**Key Concept:** You're working alone, managing both cameras and graphics.

#### Camera Switching

1. **Music/Worship:** Display song words on black background (no camera)
2. **Speaking:** Switch to Camera 1 for speakers, teaching, announcements
3. **Use Stream Deck:** Control IMAG ME directly

#### ProPresenter Operation

1. **Follow the service flow:** Stay one step ahead in the playlist
2. **Music:** Show lyrics on black screen throughout music
3. **Speaking:** Minimize text, show Camera 1
4. **Teaching slides:** Display when referenced by speaker
5. **Be ready to adapt:** Make quick decisions independently

## Skills That Help

- Comfort using computers
- Attention to timing
- Ability to follow the flow of the service

## Common Scenarios

### Streamed Services: Song/Worship

- Display lyrics keyed over the camera feed (cameras always visible in background)
- Lyrics appear as overlay on top of Director's camera mix
- Match the energy - subtle transitions for reflective songs, dynamic for upbeat
- Coordinate with Director - they're switching cameras underneath your graphics

### Streamed Services: Teaching

- Display teaching slides as lower thirds or full overlays when referenced
- Add name/title lower thirds for speakers
- Camera feed from Director always visible in background
- Coordinate timing with Director

### Streamed Services: Readings/Liturgy

- Display text keyed over the camera feed
- Time text appearance with readers
- Coordinate with Director on camera angles

### Non-Streamed Services: Song/Worship

- Display lyrics on black background (no camera feed)
- Keep text visible throughout the entire song
- Simple, clean presentation focused on words

### Non-Streamed Services: Teaching

- Show Camera 1 as the primary shot during speaking
- Display teaching slides when speaker references them
- Switch back to Camera 1 when slides are no longer needed
- Make quick decisions about when to show slides vs. camera

### Non-Streamed Services: Announcements

- Show Camera 1 for speaker
- Display announcement slides when directed
- May include video playback
