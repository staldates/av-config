# Camera Operator Role

## Overview

**Location:** Around the room - Camera 1 at the back, Camera 3 on the side, Camera 5 roving by the stage

**Primary responsibility:** Operating cameras to capture the service.

Camera operators frame shots and capture the service from different angles. They follow the Director's instructions to ensure the livestream has a variety of shots and captures key moments on stage, but they also need to use their own initiative to give the director options.

## Key Responsibilities

- Operate assigned camera during the service
- Follow the Director's instructions for framing and shots
- Communicate with the Director via comms
- Maintain awareness of what is happening on stage to anticipate shots
- Provide good shot options even when not live

## Camera Positions

### Camera 1: Back of Room

- **Position:** Stationed at the back of the room
- **Mobility:** Limited
- **Primary Role:** Close-up shots
- **Typical shots:** Tight framing on speakers, worship leaders, individuals on stage

### Camera 3: Side of Room

- **Position:** Side of the room
- **Mobility:** Limited
- **Primary Role:** Wider shots and dual coverage
- **Typical shots:** Both people when two are on stage, wider angles, medium shots

### Camera 5: Roving

- **Position:** Near the stage, mobile
- **Mobility:** High - can move to different positions as needed
- **Primary Role:** Dynamic angles and variety
- **Typical shots:** Detail shots, instruments, alternative angles
- **Privacy Note:** Avoid filming congregation for privacy reasons

## Equipment Used

- **Camera:** Assigned camera (1, 3, or 5)
- **Comms System:** Headset for Director communication
- **Camera Viewfinder:** For framing and monitoring your shots

**Note:** We don't use tally lights - this keeps the setup less distracting and allows us to capture rather than influence the service. Camera operators rely on Director's calls and their own awareness to know when they're live.

## Pre-Service Checklist

- [ ] Check camera is powered on and functioning
- [ ] Verify comms headset is working - test with Director
- [ ] Check camera viewfinder is clear and functioning
- [ ] Review service order if available
- [ ] Clear your area of obstacles (especially Camera 5)
- [ ] Set up initial framing as discussed with Director
- [ ] Confirm with Director you understand the camera assignment system

## During Service

### Following Director Instructions

The Director will call shots like:
- "Camera 1, tight on speaker"
- "Camera 5, wide worship"
- "Camera 3, prepare for tight on guitar"

**When you hear your camera number:**
1. Quickly frame the requested shot
2. Hold steady
3. Acknowledge verbally if needed ("Camera 1 ready")

### When You're Not Live

- Continue framing interesting shots
- Give the Director options to choose from
- Anticipate what might be needed next
- Stay alert and ready

### Camera Operation Best Practices

1. **Know your camera's role:**
   - Camera 1: Focus on close-ups of individuals
   - Camera 3: Provide wider shots and dual-person coverage
   - Camera 5: Dynamic variety, avoid congregation
2. **Smooth movements:** Avoid jerky pans and zooms
3. **Steady framing:** Keep shots stable - use tripod/support
4. **Headroom:** Leave appropriate space above heads
5. **Lead room:** When subject is facing sideways, leave space in front
6. **Focus:** Keep your subject in focus
7. **Horizons:** Keep horizontal lines level
8. **Anticipate:** Watch the service flow to predict shots
9. **Respect privacy:** Avoid filming congregation faces

### Shot Types

- **Wide shot:** Shows full scene/multiple people
- **Medium shot:** Waist-up or chest-up
- **Close-up:** Head and shoulders, or tighter
- **Detail shot:** Musical instruments, hands, specific objects

## Skills That Help

- Comfort operating cameras or similar equipment
- Ability to follow directions and react quickly
- Interest in video production or photography
- Steady hands and patience

## Common Scenarios

### Worship

- Mix between wide shots (Camera 2), drums (Camera 4), and worship leader close-ups
- Camera 1: Close-ups of worship leaders and vocalists
- Camera 3: Wider shots of worship team, both vocalists when duetting
- Camera 5: Detail shots of instruments, drummer's hands, guitarists
- Follow Director's lead on who to feature
- Follow the energy - more cuts during upbeat songs, hold shots during reflective moments

### Teaching/Preaching

- Camera 1: Primary close-ups of the speaker
- Camera 3: Wider shots showing context, or both people if there's an interview
- Camera 5: Alternative angles, detail shots of teaching materials
- Hold shots longer and steadier than during worship
- Anticipate when speaker might move

### Bands/Musicians

- Camera 4 (fixed) covers drums
- Camera 1: Close-ups of soloists or featured musicians
- Camera 3: Wider shots showing multiple band members
- Camera 5: Detail shots of instruments being played
- Capture energy and emotion
- Show instruments being played

### Privacy Guidelines

**Important:** For privacy reasons, avoid filming congregation members' faces:

- Camera 2 (fixed wide) naturally includes backs of heads - this is acceptable
- Operated cameras (1, 3, 5) should focus on stage, performers, and speakers
- Camera 5 should avoid panning across or zooming into congregation
- If someone is participating on stage (reading, speaking), they can be filmed

## Communication Tips

- Keep comms clear for Director instructions
- Acknowledge important calls
- Report technical issues immediately
- Stay professional and calm

## Technical Troubleshooting

### Uncertain If You're Live

- Listen carefully for Director's calls - they will tell you when you're live
- Continue framing good shots even when not live
- The Director sees all camera feeds and will guide you
- If unsure, maintain your best shot and stay ready

### Comms Not Working

- Check headset connection
- Check battery if wireless
- Alert Director immediately via another method
- Continue operating based on service flow

### Camera Issues

- Freeze/lock up: Attempt quick restart if safe to do so
- Focus problems: Switch to manual focus
- Any major issue: Alert Director immediately
