# Camera Setup

## Camera Assignments

### Fixed Cameras

#### Camera 2: Wide Shot

- **Position:** Fixed
- **Coverage:** Wide shot of the stage/room (includes backs of congregation heads)
- **Purpose:** Establishing shots, full stage coverage
- **Use:** Good for worship team, full congregation view, scene-setting, safety shot

#### Camera 4: Drums

- **Position:** Fixed
- **Coverage:** Drum kit
- **Purpose:** Drummer coverage
- **Use:** Dynamic shots during worship, drum solos, rhythm section focus

#### Camera 6: Livestream Hosts

- **Position:** Fixed, outside the main room
- **Coverage:** Livestream host position
- **Purpose:** Online host segments, welcome messages, announcements
- **Use:** Pre-service, transitions, online-specific content

### Operated Cameras

#### Camera 1: Back of Room

- **Position:** Back of the room (operated)
- **Operator Role:** Follow Director's instructions, primarily close-ups
- **Coverage:** Front-facing shots of stage and speakers, tight framing
- **Typical Use:** Close-up shots of speakers, worship leaders, individuals on stage
- **Mobility:** Limited - stationed at back

#### Camera 3: Side of Room

- **Position:** Side of the room (operated)
- **Operator Role:** Follow Director's instructions, wider shots and dual coverage
- **Coverage:** Side perspective of stage, alternate angles
- **Typical Use:** Wider shots, both people when two are on stage, medium shots
- **Mobility:** Limited - stationed at side

#### Camera 5: Roving

- **Position:** Near the stage (operated)
- **Operator Role:** Follow Director's instructions, high mobility
- **Coverage:** Close-ups, dynamic shots, detail shots
- **Privacy Note:** Avoid congregation shots for privacy reasons
- **Mobility:** High - can move to capture different angles and moments

## Camera Operation Guidelines

### For Camera Operators

1. **Listen to the Director:** Follow instructions via comms for framing and shot preparation
2. **Know your camera's role:**
   - Camera 1: Focus on close-ups
   - Camera 3: Provide wider shots and dual-person coverage
   - Camera 5: Dynamic angles, avoid congregation for privacy
3. **Anticipate shots:** Watch the service flow to predict what might be needed next
4. **Frame properly:** Leave headroom, follow rule of thirds when appropriate
5. **Stay steady:** Use smooth movements, avoid sudden jerks
6. **Respect privacy:** Avoid filming congregation faces (except Camera 2's wide shots)
7. **Give options:** When not actively live, frame interesting shots for the Director to choose from

### For the Director

1. **Call shots clearly:** Give camera operators time to prepare the next shot
2. **Use cameras by role:**
   - Camera 1 for close-ups of individuals
   - Camera 3 for wider shots and when two people are on stage
   - Camera 5 for variety and movement (avoid congregation)
3. **Vary your angles:** Mix wide shots, medium shots, and close-ups
4. **Follow the action:** Switch to cameras that capture what's currently important
5. **Use fixed cameras strategically:** Camera 2 (wide) for safety, Camera 4 (drums) for energy
6. **Respect privacy:** Be mindful when using congregation shots
7. **Communicate:** Let camera operators know what's coming next in the service
